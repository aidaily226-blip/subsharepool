## Keeping Your Netflix Account Secure While Sharing

Security is the number one concern people have about sharing subscription accounts — and rightfully so. Here's how to protect yourself:

### Use separate Netflix profiles

Netflix allows up to 5 individual profiles per account. Each member should have their own dedicated profile with a profile PIN enabled. This keeps watch histories, recommendation algorithms, and download queues completely separate. No one can see what the other is watching.

### Never share your account password directly

This is where platforms like [SubSharePool](https://subsharepool.com/?tab=subs) add real value. Rather than texting your password to friends — which is a security risk — the platform manages access through its own verification system. The account owner retains full control of the Netflix credentials.

### Enable two-factor authentication

Make sure your Netflix account email and payment method have 2FA enabled. This ensures that even if someone knows your Netflix password, they can't change account settings or payment details without your phone.

### Review account activity regularly

Netflix lets you view all active sessions and devices from **Account Settings → Recent Device Streaming Activity**. Check this monthly to make sure only your authorized pool members are using the account.

> ⚠️ **Important Note:** Netflix's password sharing policy has evolved. Always use the official Extra Member feature where available in your country — it keeps your account compliant with Netflix's terms and avoids any service interruption.
